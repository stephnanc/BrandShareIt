Festival Foods presents a critical pricing concern: cases shipped increased 50% (5,299 to 7,928) while sales dollars declined 34% ($11.2K to $7.4K), indicating severe pricing erosion or heavy promotional activity.

Average revenue per case dropped dramatically from $2.11 to $0.93 (a 56% decline), suggesting either significant price reductions, shift to lower-priced SKUs, or excessive trade spending.

21 of 29 locations (72%) experienced sales declines despite shipping more cases, with catastrophic drops in Appleton (-69%), Janesville (-70%), and Manitowoc (-62%).

The disconnect between volume growth and revenue decline requires immediate investigation into pricing strategy, promotional terms, deductions, and potential data accuracy issues with UNFI reporting.

Only 5 stores showed positive sales growth (Sheboygan +37%, Verona +19%, West Allis +18%), suggesting localized market conditions or execution differences.

This portfolio demands urgent strategic review to understand root causes: Are we selling at unsustainably low prices? Are deductions or chargebacks distorting the numbers? Is product mix shifting unfavorably?
