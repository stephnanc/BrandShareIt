A full analytics summary review across all KeHE time periods (Month to Date, Year to Date, Last 13 Weeks, Last 26 Weeks, Last 12 Months, and Last 52 Weeks) was completed this week. Below are the key highlights.

Fill Rate — The Standout Story

Swoffle's outbound fill rate has improved dramatically across every time period reviewed. The most compelling number is the Last 26 Weeks at 95.12% — up 21.16 points vs. the same period last year (73.96%). Over the full 52-week trailing period, fill rate sits at 88.13%, up 11.41 points year over year. This represents a significant operational turnaround and is our strongest talking point heading into any KeHE category review.

Distribution Growth in 2026

PODs (Points of Distribution) are showing meaningful recovery in the shorter time windows. Month to Date we are up 22.86% vs. prior year, and up 13.71% over the Last 13 Weeks. This early-2026 door growth is an encouraging reversal after PODs trended slightly negative over the full 12-month and 52-week views.

KeHE Buying Activity
Click to View — KeHE Analytics Dashboard

Purchases through KeHE are up across every time period — most notably up 128.07% over the Last 13 Weeks ($23,602 vs. $10,349 PY) and up 68.05% Year to Date. KeHE is actively investing in stocking Swoffle, which is a strong signal of distributor confidence in the brand.

Sales COGS — Watch Item

Sales COGS is running below prior year across all time periods, ranging from a 6.30% decline over the Last 13 Weeks to 12.45% over the Last 52 Weeks. Positively, the gap is narrowing in the shorter windows, which suggests the trend is beginning to recover. The primary driver of this decline is the 16-pack to 12-pack SKU transition, which has also significantly impacted SKU Productivity metrics (down approximately 47–60% across all periods). This is expected to normalize as the 12-pack matures in distribution and velocity builds at the store level.

Deductions

Total deductions remain at $7,254 (13.17% of COGS), with retailer-side deductions representing 60% of the total burden. This is a focus area for upcoming KeHE account conversations.

Overall Trajectory

The directional story is improving. Fill rate, PODs, and purchases are all trending positively. The COGS gap is narrowing. As the 12-pack SKU continues to establish velocity across Swoffle's 523 KeHE doors, the expectation is that revenue and SKU productivity metrics will follow the distribution and fill rate gains already being realized.
