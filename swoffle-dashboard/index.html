This breaks down revenue sources between distributor partnerships and direct-to-customer sales. Understanding channel performance helps optimize resource allocation and identify the most profitable sales strategies. It reveals which channels are driving growth and where to focus expansion efforts.

This measures how fast each sales channel is growing over time. It helps identify which channels are gaining traction and which may need attention or optimization. Comparing growth rates across channels guides strategic decisions about where to invest resources.

Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) â€º

This tracks predictable revenue that repeats each month from ongoing orders or subscriptions. For B2B sales, this includes regular reorders from established accounts. High MRR indicates strong customer relationships and business stability.

Revenue per Account â€º

This calculates the average revenue generated from each customer account. It helps identify high-value accounts and opportunities to increase sales to existing customers. Growing this metric indicates successful account expansion and upselling strategies.

Average Order Value (AOV) â€º

This measures the typical dollar amount of each order placed by customers. Increasing AOV through bundling, promotions, or product mix optimization directly impacts revenue without acquiring new customers. It's a key indicator of sales effectiveness and customer buying behavior.

Year-over-Year Revenue Comparison â€º

This compares current period revenue directly against the same period in the previous year. It accounts for seasonal variations and provides clear insight into business trajectory. This comparison is essential for setting realistic forecasts and identifying areas requiring strategic attention.

Total Active Customers/Accounts â€º

This counts all customers who have made purchases within a defined recent period (typically 90-180 days). It represents your current customer base and market reach. Tracking this number over time shows whether you're successfully expanding your market presence.

New Customers Acquired â€º

This tracks the number of brand-new customers who made their first purchase during a specific period. It measures the effectiveness of your marketing and sales efforts to expand market share. Consistent new customer acquisition is essential for long-term growth.

Customer Acquisition Rate â€º

This calculates the percentage increase in your customer base over time. It shows how quickly you're growing your market presence relative to your existing customer base. A healthy acquisition rate indicates effective sales and marketing strategies.

Customer Retention Rate â€º

This measures the percentage of customers who continue to purchase from you over time. High retention rates indicate strong customer satisfaction and product-market fit. Retaining existing customers is typically more cost-effective than acquiring new ones.

Customer Churn Rate â€º

This tracks the percentage of customers who stop purchasing within a given period. It's the inverse of retention rate and signals potential issues with product quality, service, or competitive positioning. Reducing churn is critical for sustainable growth.

Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) â€º

This estimates the total revenue a customer will generate throughout their entire relationship with your business. It helps determine how much you can afford to spend on customer acquisition while remaining profitable. High CLV customers should receive focused retention efforts.

Net Customer Growth â€º

This calculates the net change in customer count by subtracting lost customers from new customers acquired. It provides a clear picture of whether your customer base is actually growing or shrinking. Positive net growth indicates healthy business expansion.
