üí∞ Revenue & Growth Metrics
Total Revenue (Monthly/Quarterly/Annual) ‚Ä∫

This measures the total income generated from all sales activities over a specific time period. It's your company's top-line number and the foundation for understanding overall business performance. Tracking this across different time frames helps identify seasonal trends and growth patterns.

Revenue Growth Rate (YoY %) ‚Ä∫

This calculates the percentage increase or decrease in revenue compared to the same period last year. It's a critical indicator of business health and market position. Consistent positive growth rates demonstrate strong business momentum and competitive advantage.

Revenue by Channel (Distributor vs Direct) ‚Ä∫

This breaks down revenue sources between distributor partnerships and direct-to-customer sales. Understanding channel performance helps optimize resource allocation and identify the most profitable sales strategies. It reveals which channels are driving growth and where to focus expansion efforts.

Channel Growth Rate (% growth by channel) ‚Ä∫

This measures how fast each sales channel is growing over time. It helps identify which channels are gaining traction and which may need attention or optimization. Comparing growth rates across channels guides strategic decisions about where to invest resources.

Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) ‚Ä∫

This tracks predictable revenue that repeats each month from ongoing orders or subscriptions. For B2B sales, this includes regular reorders from established accounts. High MRR indicates strong customer relationships and business stability.

Revenue per Account ‚Ä∫

This calculates the average revenue generated from each customer account. It helps identify high-value accounts and opportunities to increase sales to existing customers. Growing this metric indicates successful account expansion and upselling strategies.

Average Order Value (AOV) ‚Ä∫

This measures the typical dollar amount of each order placed by customers. Increasing AOV through bundling, promotions, or product mix optimization directly impacts revenue without acquiring new customers. It's a key indicator of sales effectiveness and customer buying behavior.

Year-over-Year Revenue Comparison ‚Ä∫

This compares current period revenue directly against the same period in the previous year. It accounts for seasonal variations and provides clear insight into business trajectory. This comparison is essential for setting realistic forecasts and identifying areas requiring strategic attention.

üë• Customer Metrics
Total Active Customers/Accounts ‚Ä∫

This counts all customers who have made purchases within a defined recent period (typically 90-180 days). It represents your current customer base and market reach. Tracking this number over time shows whether you're successfully expanding your market presence.

New Customers Acquired ‚Ä∫

This tracks the number of brand-new customers who made their first purchase during a specific period. It measures the effectiveness of your marketing and sales efforts to expand market share. Consistent new customer acquisition is essential for long-term growth.

Customer Acquisition Rate ‚Ä∫

This calculates the percentage increase in your customer base over time. It shows how quickly you're growing your market presence relative to your existing customer base. A healthy acquisition rate indicates effective sales and marketing strategies.

Customer Retention Rate ‚Ä∫

This measures the percentage of customers who continue to purchase from you over time. High retention rates indicate strong customer satisfaction and product-market fit. Retaining existing customers is typically more cost-effective than acquiring new ones.

Customer Churn Rate ‚Ä∫

This tracks the percentage of customers who stop purchasing within a given period. It's the inverse of retention rate and signals potential issues with product quality, service, or competitive positioning. Reducing churn is critical for sustainable growth.

Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) ‚Ä∫

This estimates the total revenue a customer will generate throughout their entire relationship with your business. It helps determine how much you can afford to spend on customer acquisition while remaining profitable. High CLV customers should receive focused retention efforts.

Net Customer Growth ‚Ä∫

This calculates the net change in customer count by subtracting lost customers from new customers acquired. It provides a clear picture of whether your customer base is actually growing or shrinking. Positive net growth indicates healthy business expansion.

This measures the total revenue generated by each individual sales representative over a specific period. It helps identify top performers and those who may need additional training or support. Tracking this metric ensures equitable territory distribution and fair compensation structures.

Accounts per Sales Rep ‚Ä∫

This tracks the number of active customer accounts managed by each sales representative. It reveals workload distribution across your team and helps optimize territory assignments. Balancing account load ensures reps can provide adequate attention to each customer while maximizing coverage.

New Accounts per Rep ‚Ä∫

This counts how many new customer accounts each sales rep successfully brings onboard during a given timeframe. It measures individual contribution to business expansion and new market penetration. High performers in this area demonstrate strong prospecting and closing skills.

Average Deal Size per Rep ‚Ä∫

This calculates the typical value of deals closed by each sales representative. It identifies reps who excel at selling to larger accounts or upselling products. Understanding deal size patterns helps align reps with appropriate customer segments and opportunities.

This measures the percentage of assigned sales quotas that each rep actually achieves. It provides clear visibility into individual performance against expectations and helps identify coaching needs. Consistent achievement above 100% indicates strong performers who may be ready for bigger territories or leadership roles.

Sales Cycle Length ‚Ä∫

This measures the average time from initial contact to closed deal. Shorter cycles indicate efficient sales processes and strong product-market fit. Monitoring this metric helps identify bottlenecks in your sales process and opportunities to accelerate revenue generation.

Activity Metrics (Calls, Emails, Meetings) ‚Ä∫

These track the volume of outreach activities performed by each sales rep. Activity levels correlate strongly with pipeline development and revenue generation. Consistent tracking helps ensure reps maintain adequate prospecting efforts while balancing existing account management.

üíé Profitability Metrics
Gross Profit Margin (Overall %) ‚Ä∫

This calculates the percentage of revenue remaining after subtracting cost of goods sold. It's a fundamental indicator of business health and pricing effectiveness. Declining margins may signal pricing pressure, rising costs, or unfavorable product mix shifts.

Gross Profit by Channel ‚Ä∫

This breaks down profit margins across different sales channels like distributors, direct sales, or online. Different channels typically have varying cost structures and margin profiles. Understanding channel profitability helps optimize your go-to-market strategy and resource allocation.

Profit per Customer ‚Ä∫

This measures the average profit generated from each customer account. It helps identify your most valuable customers beyond just revenue volume. High-profit customers should receive prioritized service and retention efforts to protect this critical revenue base.

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) ‚Ä∫

This tracks the direct costs associated with producing or purchasing products sold. Monitoring COGS trends helps identify opportunities for supplier negotiations or production efficiencies. Rising COGS relative to revenue indicates margin compression that requires strategic action.

Sales & Marketing Expenses ‚Ä∫

This measures total spending on sales team compensation, marketing campaigns, and customer acquisition activities. Analyzing these costs as a percentage of revenue reveals efficiency of your go-to-market investments. Optimizing this ratio while maintaining growth is key to sustainable profitability.

Return on Sales (ROS) ‚Ä∫

This calculates net profit as a percentage of total revenue, showing how much profit you generate per dollar of sales. It's a comprehensive profitability metric that accounts for all operating expenses. Strong ROS indicates effective cost management and competitive pricing power.

Revenue by Product Category ‚Ä∫

This breaks down sales across your different product lines or categories. It reveals which product groups drive the most revenue and where growth opportunities exist. Understanding category performance guides inventory decisions, marketing focus, and new product development priorities.

Top 10 Products by Revenue ‚Ä∫

This ranks your best-selling individual products by total revenue generated. It helps you identify star performers that deserve promotional investment and stock prioritization. These top products often represent your strongest competitive advantages and customer value propositions.

Product Growth Rate ‚Ä∫

This measures sales growth for each product or category over time. Fast-growing products indicate strong market demand and may warrant increased production or inventory investment. Declining products may need revitalization strategies or eventual phase-out decisions.

New Product Revenue ‚Ä∫

This tracks sales generated by recently launched products within their first year. It measures innovation success and market acceptance of new offerings. Strong new product revenue indicates effective product development and launch execution capabilities.

Product Mix Analysis ‚Ä∫

This examines the relative proportion of sales across your entire product portfolio. Understanding product mix helps optimize pricing strategies and identify cross-selling opportunities. Shifts in mix can significantly impact overall profitability even when total revenue remains stable.

Cross-Sell/Up-Sell Rate ‚Ä∫

This measures how often customers purchase additional or premium products beyond their initial order. High rates indicate effective sales techniques and complementary product positioning. Improving this metric directly increases revenue per customer without additional acquisition costs.

‚ö° Operational Efficiency
Order Frequency ‚Ä∫

This tracks how often customers place orders within a given timeframe. Higher frequency indicates strong product-market fit and reliable customer relationships. Monitoring changes in order frequency provides early warning of satisfaction issues or competitive threats.

Reorder Rate ‚Ä∫

This measures the percentage of customers who make repeat purchases after their initial order. High reorder rates indicate customer satisfaction and successful product delivery. It's a leading indicator of customer retention and lifetime value potential.

Quote-to-Close Ratio ‚Ä∫

This calculates the percentage of quotes or proposals that convert into actual sales. A low ratio may indicate pricing issues, poor qualification, or competitive disadvantages. Improving this metric increases revenue without requiring additional prospecting effort.

Sales Productivity ‚Ä∫

This measures revenue generated per sales employee or per hour of selling time. It reveals how efficiently your sales team converts effort into results. Improving productivity through better tools, training, or processes directly impacts profitability without increasing headcount.

Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) ‚Ä∫

This tracks the average number of days it takes to collect payment after a sale. Lower DSO improves cash flow and reduces financing costs. High DSO may indicate credit policy issues or customer financial difficulties requiring attention.

Inventory Turnover ‚Ä∫

This measures how many times inventory is sold and replaced during a period. Higher turnover indicates efficient inventory management and strong sales velocity. Low turnover ties up capital and increases storage costs and obsolescence risk.

üîÆ Pipeline & Forecasting

Qualified Leads ‚Ä∫

This counts prospects who meet your criteria for budget, authority, need, and timeline. Tracking qualified leads helps predict future sales and assess marketing effectiveness. A steady flow of qualified leads is essential for consistent revenue growth.

Conversion Rate ‚Ä∫

This measures the percentage of leads that progress through each stage of your sales funnel. Understanding conversion rates at each stage identifies bottlenecks in your sales process. Improving conversion rates directly increases revenue without requiring more leads.

Forecast Accuracy ‚Ä∫

This compares predicted sales against actual results to measure forecasting reliability. Accurate forecasting enables better resource planning and inventory management. Improving forecast accuracy reduces waste and missed opportunities from over or under-preparation.

Pipeline Velocity ‚Ä∫

This calculates how quickly opportunities move through your sales pipeline to closure. Faster velocity means revenue is realized sooner and sales capacity is freed for new opportunities. Slow velocity may indicate process inefficiencies or poor lead quality.

üòä Customer Satisfaction
Net Promoter Score (NPS) ‚Ä∫

This measures customer willingness to recommend your business to others on a -100 to +100 scale. High NPS correlates with strong customer loyalty and organic growth through referrals. It's one of the most widely used indicators of overall customer satisfaction.

Customer Satisfaction Score ‚Ä∫

This captures direct customer ratings of their satisfaction with your products and services. Regular satisfaction tracking helps identify issues before they result in churn. Improving satisfaction scores typically leads to higher retention and increased lifetime value.

Customer Complaint Rate ‚Ä∫

This tracks the frequency of customer complaints relative to total transactions or customers. Rising complaint rates signal quality or service issues requiring immediate attention. Proactive complaint management prevents customer loss and protects brand reputation.

Resolution Time ‚Ä∫

This measures how quickly customer issues or complaints are fully resolved. Fast resolution demonstrates commitment to customer service and helps maintain relationships despite problems. Long resolution times damage trust and increase churn risk even when issues are eventually fixed.

Repeat Purchase Rate ‚Ä∫

This calculates the percentage of customers who make multiple purchases over time. High repeat rates indicate customer satisfaction and successful relationship building. This metric is closely tied to customer lifetime value and overall business sustainability.

This measures your percentage of total sales within your target market or category. Growing market share indicates competitive strength even in flat markets. Understanding share trends helps assess whether your growth comes from market expansion or winning competitor customers.

Competitive Win Rate ‚Ä∫

This tracks the percentage of competitive opportunities where you win the business. High win rates indicate strong competitive positioning and value proposition. Analyzing losses reveals where competitors have advantages you need to address.
