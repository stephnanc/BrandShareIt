Marketing Programs & Promotions

▼

Marketing Overview

UNFI's marketing programs build brand awareness, advertise promotions, educate store buyers, and target specific channels. UNFI expects Supplier participation and collaboration.

Promotional Planning

UNFI Expects:
  • Annual promotional plans (off invoice, MCB, or CMAP)
  • Preferably quarterly promotions per product group
  • Appropriate documentation for all promotional activities

Promotional Inventory Policies

  • Excess Product: Credit UNFI for difference between current inventory price and promotional discount
  • Insufficient Product: Credit required (deducted automatically)
  • Unable to Ship Full Quantity: Credit for full amount ordered
  • Deal match within total distribution network (unless agreed otherwise)

MCB (Manufacturer Charge Back)

Unless agreed with SRM, UNFI does not accept promotions based solely on MCBs. UNFI calculates MCBs based on wholesale catalog pricing.

EDLP (Natural DCs Only)

  • Customer-specific deals, minimum 6 months
  • Submit on UNFI EDLP form 10 business days prior
  • Chain-wide application
  • 60-day notice to end "ongoing" EDLPs
  • Submitted as MCB percentage discounts

Turnover Orders (Natural DCs Only)

One-time deals for basic stocking, line extension, promotion, demos, store expansion, new store opening, new product placement. Submit on Turnover Form via email:

  • East: TurnoversEast@unfi.com
  • West: TurnoversWest@unfi.com

Customer Support Programs

  • Opening Orders: Free fills for new products/stores at wholesale price
  • Reclamations: Supplier charged for customer reclamation costs (spoils, returns, shelf worn)
  • Resets: Proportional cost of retail services for categories being reset
Pro Tip: Plan quarterly promotions in advance. Ensure adequate inventory during promotional periods to avoid automatic credit deductions. Monitor promotional performance to qualify for UNFI's top "invitation only" programs.

Product Requirements & Specifications

▼

New Product Setup

  • Submit via e-New Item in SVHarbor or Supplier Portal
  • Per-SKU setup fee for each DC activation
  • No new products accepted until old products returned

Guaranteed Sale - 6 Months

New Products (except fresh produce/protein):
  • 6-month guaranteed sale on initial POs per DC
  • Includes reactivated products
  • Supplier responsible for excess/unsold inventory

Product Specification Changes

90-Day Notice Required for:
  • Formulations (UPC, size, pack)
  • Labels, packaging
  • Organic status changes

UPC changes require recode. Email: supplierpackchanges@unfi.com

Discontinued Products

UNFI will provide inventory count, arrange return/disposal. Supplier must:

  • Make disposition arrangements within 14 days (customer discontinuation) or 30 days (UNFI discontinuation)
  • Provide full refund or arrange pickup
  • If not arranged, UNFI disposes and charges full landed cost plus fees

Private-Label Products

If customer ceases purchasing private-label products, UNFI may cancel outstanding POs and/or return inventory to Supplier for full refund.

Quality Standards, Certifications & Date Codes

▼

Quality Assurance

Mandatory:
  • Good Manufacturing Practices (GMPs)
  • Meet USDA or PACA quality guidelines
  • Annual third-party food safety audits (if selling food)

Immediate Notification Required

  • Abnormal laboratory results
  • Third-party audits indicating pathogens requiring recall
  • Government inquiries/inspections
  • Registration changes (PACA, FDA, USDA FSIS)

Certifications - Annual Renewal

Organic USDA NOP standards. Send to: foodsafetycertificates@unfi.com
Kosher Recognized certifying agency. Include Supplier name & UPC.
Non-GMO Non-GMO Project or equivalent.
Fair Trade Fair Trade USA or equivalent.

Date Codes & Shelf Life

Critical 75% Rule:
  • Products must have ≥75% of production shelf life remaining at DC receipt
  • Open coded shelf life or "use by" date required on product and shipping case
  • Supplier solely responsible for shelf-life compliance
  • UNFI will charge back products not sold before expiration

Legal Compliance

  • Pesticides: EPA/state registration at Supplier's cost
  • HAZMAT: Register with WERCS, provide safety data sheets
  • Prop 65: Provide all required warnings
  • PFAS: Comply with all PFAS laws
  • Traceability: Unique lot/batch numbers, FSMA 204 compliance

SSA Program Overview, Benefits & Scorecard

▼

Purpose: The Simplified Supplier Approach provides Suppliers with data insights, consolidates fees, and waives compliance fees when performance requirements are met.

Program Scope & Eligibility

Eligible Suppliers:
  • Annual purchases by UNFI of $25,000+ in previous four quarters
  • Supplying to UNFI for one year or more
SSA Does NOT Apply To:
  • UNFI Canada products for Canadian market
  • Private label/UNFI-owned brands
  • Military program products
  • International division "SVI" products
  • Suppliers with ClearVue/Corporate/ESA Agreements

SSA Benefits

1. Fee Waivers DCE fees, slotting fees (new item setup only), SVHarbor access fees, ReposiTrak fees
2. UNFI Insights™ Access Proprietary reporting platform for product performance data and analytics
3. Compliance Fee Waivers When "green" thresholds met: appointment, load, service, costing, and late notification fees waived

Simplified Fee: 2.5%

  • Natural Channel: Apply to bottom of each invoice
  • Conventional Channel: Deducted quarterly by UNFI
  • Calculated on total purchases over preceding 4 quarters

Supplier Scorecard - Performance Requirements

Five "Fee Buckets" tracked monthly:

Green Threshold Requirements:
  1. Appointment Violations: ≤5% violations
  2. Load Conditions: ≤5% violations
  3. Costing Letters/Invoice Surcharge: ≤5% violations
  4. Service Level: ≥95% fill rate
  5. Late Notification/Disposition: ≤1% violations

Warning System

  • Month 1: Review for extenuating circumstances, formal warning if none apply
  • Months 2-3: Continued warnings at 60 and 90 days
  • After 3 Consecutive Months: Compliance fee waivers revoked for subsequent month
  • Reinstatement: Achieve "green" threshold to restore waivers
Pro Tip: Monitor your monthly scorecard closely. Set internal alerts when any metric approaches threshold limits to prevent three consecutive warnings.

Supplier Setup, Onboarding, Products & Quality

Quality Standards, Certifications & Date Codes

▼

Quality Assurance

Mandatory:
  • Good Manufacturing Practices (GMPs)
  • Meet USDA or PACA quality guidelines
  • Annual third-party food safety audits (if selling food)

Immediate Notification Required

  • Abnormal laboratory results
  • Third-party audits indicating pathogens requiring recall
  • Government inquiries/inspections
  • Registration changes (PACA, FDA, USDA FSIS)

Certifications - Annual Renewal

Organic USDA NOP standards. Send to: foodsafetycertificates@unfi.com
Kosher Recognized certifying agency. Include Supplier name & UPC.
Non-GMO Non-GMO Project or equivalent.
Fair Trade Fair Trade USA or equivalent.

Date Codes & Shelf Life

Critical 75% Rule:
  • Products must have ≥75% of production shelf life remaining at DC receipt
  • Open coded shelf life or "use by" date required on product and shipping case
  • Supplier solely responsible for shelf-life compliance
  • UNFI will charge back products not sold before expiration

Legal Compliance

  • Pesticides: EPA/state registration at Supplier's cost
  • HAZMAT: Register with WERCS, provide safety data sheets
  • Prop 65: Provide all required warnings
  • PFAS: Comply with all PFAS laws
  • Traceability: Unique lot/batch numbers, FSMA 204 compliance

Deductions

  • UNFI pays invoices net of deductions
  • 12-month window to dispute deductions
  • Email disputes to: SupplierDeductionDisputeMgmt@unfi.com

Price Changes - 90-Day Notice

Requirements:
  • 90 days prior written notice (except fresh protein/produce/dairy)
  • Effective with UNFI PO order date
  • No price changes on products during promotion
  • Price Decreases: Price protection required on all affected inventory
  • Price Increases: UNFI may forward buy

Favorable Terms Clause

Pricing/promotional terms must be at least as favorable as offered to any other distributor buying similar products/volumes. More favorable terms automatically apply to UNFI.

Shipping & Receiving Requirements

▼

Supplier must comply with UNFI's Shipping and Handling Policy.

Key Requirements

  • Appointments: 24+ hours advance via www.myunfi.com
  • Lumper Fees: Use UNFI-contracted lumper services
  • Pallets: GMA 4-Way 40"x48", shrink-wrapped, no overhang
  • Temperature Controls: Must be stated on cases, temperature data recorder required
  • Barcodes: Scannable on all shipping cases
  • Documentation: 2 copies each of BOL & packing slip

UNFI Pick Up vs Prepaid Delivery

UNFI Pick Up UNFI picks up at Supplier's dock. 90-day notice for location changes. Subject to count at DC.
Prepaid Delivery Supplier ships FOB destination. Must schedule appointment. Supplier responsible for late/unscheduled fees.

Service Level - 95% Fill Rate

Mandatory:
  • Maintain ≥95% fill rate
  • If below for 2+ consecutive weeks: corrective action plan required
  • Failure may result in fees and/or product discontinuation

Out of Stocks - 4+ Weeks

Submit dated, signed letter on letterhead to SRM including:

  • Brand name, UPC, description
  • Root cause
  • Estimated resolution time

Mis-Ships/Over-Ships

  • Supplier responsible for all associated costs
  • If not picked up within 14 days, UNFI disposes and charges landed cost plus fees

Unacceptable Products & Recalls

▼

Unacceptable Product Definition

Any product that is:
  1. Temperature-sensitive without data recorder or exceeded temperature limits
  2. Shelf life <75% upon UNFI receipt
  3. Unable to maintain quality/integrity throughout shelf life
  4. Does not conform to specifications or is defective
  5. Unsaleable (customer returns, shelf worn, etc.)
  6. Subject to recall or market withdrawal

UNFI's Rights

  • Refuse delivery of unacceptable products
  • Return products for full credit, freight paid by Supplier
  • Dispose at Supplier's expense if sale/disposal price less than UNFI's cost

Recalls & Product Withdrawals

IMMEDIATE NOTIFICATION REQUIRED:
  • Email: recalls@unfi.com
  • Notify UNFI within 6 hours of decision to recall
  • Make every effort to notify before public announcement
  • Fully cooperate with UNFI
  • Comply with UNFI's Recall & Withdrawal Policy

Recall Costs - Supplier Responsibility

UNFI will charge Supplier for all fees including:

  • Customer fees related to recall
  • Product retrieval from retail stores
  • Storage, shipping, disposal costs
  • Communication expenses (press releases, government advisory, warning letters)

Insurance Requirements

▼

Supplier must provide Certificate of Insurance (COI) annually from carrier with A.M. Best rating A-, Financial Size category VII.

Required Coverage

Commercial General Liability:

For supplements, meats, or seafood:

  • $5,000,000 combined single limit per occurrence
  • $5,000,000 products/completed operations aggregate
  • $5,000,000 general aggregate

For other products:

  • $1,000,000 combined single limit per occurrence
  • $2,000,000 products/completed operations aggregate
  • $2,000,000 general aggregate
Workers' Compensation:

Statutory coverage as required by law

Employer's Liability:

$100,000 per accident for bodily injury or disease; $1,000,000 aggregate for disease

Commercial Automobile Liability:

$1,000,000 combined single limit covering bodily injury and property damage for owned, non-owned, leased, and hired autos

Additional Requirements

  • Designate "United Natural Foods, Inc. and its affiliates" as additional insureds on primary non-contributory basis
  • Contractual liability insurance covering Supplier's obligations to defend and indemnify UNFI
  • Waiver of subrogation in favor of UNFI and affiliates
  • 30-day written notice of modification, cancellation, or termination
  • Coverage maintained minimum 1 year following last shipment or agreement termination

Certificate Submission

Send renewal certificates to: records@unfi.com

CRITICAL: Failure to provide current COI may result in disruption of service and prevent UNFI from purchasing from Supplier.

Legal Provisions & Other Requirements

▼

Press Release Policy

Prior Written Consent Required:

Without UNFI's written consent, Supplier may not make public announcements, press releases, advertisements, social media postings, or marketing materials that refer to:

  • UNFI
  • UNFI's purchase of Products from Supplier
  • Subject matter of any agreement with UNFI

Cyber Security

Supplier responsible for:

  • Integrity of own IT system
  • Implementing commercially reasonable processes to prevent cyber breaches
  • Any damages incurred by UNFI from Supplier's systems or failure to maintain security

Sales Support

Upon UNFI request and at no additional cost, Supplier will provide reasonable sales support. Level and type mutually agreed in writing.

Product Samples Policy

In limited circumstances, UNFI may pull product samples from inventory to introduce products to select customers or photograph for publications. Processed as chargeback or MCB at regular wholesale price.

Title and Risk of Loss

Title and risk pass to UNFI on pick-up at Supplier's dock or upon delivery to UNFI-designated destination. All shipments subject to final inspection and approval.

Audit Rights

Upon written notice, UNFI may audit Supplier's or Supplier's vendors/co-packers' facilities. For food safety issues or recalls, Supplier covers all audit costs.

Product Information & Intellectual Property

Supplier grants UNFI worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use Product information and IP (trademarks, images, labels, etc.) for marketing and sale of Products.

Global Data Sync Preferred:

Provide via GDSN connection or syndication provider (IX-One, Syndigo, 1WorldSync). Contact: DataSync@unfi.com

Case Splitting

UNFI may charge fee for grocery items case split in DCs to allow one shelf facing at retail. Does not apply to inner packs or each pick items.

Inventory Transfers

To meet customer service levels, UNFI may transfer or require Supplier to transfer products between DCs. Supplier responsible for associated costs.

Pro Tip: Maintain comprehensive insurance coverage and update certificates promptly before expiration. Keep detailed food safety records and third-party audit reports readily accessible. Establish clear internal protocols for recalls - speed of response is critical. Review cyber security measures regularly to protect both your business and UNFI.
