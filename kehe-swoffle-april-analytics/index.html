Understanding SKU Productivity — Why It's Down ~50% & What It Means
The 16-pack to 12-pack transition explained  |  Click to expand
▼

SKU Productivity in KeHE's analytics system is a measure of how much revenue each individual SKU generates per point of distribution. Think of it this way: if Swoffle has 5 SKUs listed at KeHE and they're in 100 stores, that's 500 "opportunities" to sell. SKU Productivity tells you how efficiently those opportunities are being converted into actual dollars. A high number means your SKUs are working hard — they're in doors and selling through. A low number means either the SKUs aren't moving well at the store level, or SKUs are occupying distribution slots without generating meaningful volume.

Why Swoffle's number dropped ~50% across every time period comes down to two things happening simultaneously. First, the 16-pack to 12-pack transition. When a pack size changes, KeHE's system treats the new 12-pack as a different SKU. For a period of time you have the old SKU losing velocity as it phases out, and the new SKU building from zero — both dragging the average down hard at the same time. Second, during a pack change transition there is often a window where PODs are counted but sell-through hasn't caught up yet. The brand is in the door, but the new pack is still finding its footing with shoppers and store staff. That gap between distribution and velocity crushes the productivity metric temporarily.

The good news is that SKU productivity is one of the most recoverable metrics once the transition dust settles. As the 12-pack establishes velocity in those 523 doors, the number should start climbing back. The POD gains visible in the shorter time windows — up 13–22% in Q1 2026 — are actually a leading indicator that productivity should follow. More doors filled with a settled, well-positioned SKU means higher productivity scores ahead.

Talking point for David Shaffer: Frame the SKU productivity decline as a transition artifact tied to the pack change, not a demand or shelf performance problem. The supporting evidence — fill rate up 11–21 points, purchases up 5–128% depending on window, and POD recovery in Q1 2026 — all argue that the brand's fundamentals are intact and the productivity metric will normalize as the 12-pack matures in distribution.
